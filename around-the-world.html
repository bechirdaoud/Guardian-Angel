7-year circumnavigation concept

Around the World

A season-aware route designed to avoid the main cyclone/hurricane windows and maximise time in the best cruising grounds. This is a sample plan you can refine as the project evolves.

Mediterranean: May–Oct Atlantic hurricanes: Jun–Nov South Pacific cyclones: Nov–Apr Australia cyclones: Nov–Apr SW Indian Ocean cyclones: mid‑Nov–Apr

Structure

Each year is split into a “prime sailing season” block and a “maintenance / weather avoidance” block. The itinerary deliberately uses the same strategy most ocean-rally routes use: cross oceans during established windows and spend cyclone seasons outside the tropics.

  • Year 1: Mediterranean test season + winter refit (Europe)
  • Year 2: Mediterranean + Atlantic crossing + Caribbean season
  • Year 3: Panama + Galápagos + French Polynesia + cyclone season in NZ
  • Year 4: Australia + Indonesia + SE Asia
  • Year 5: Indian Ocean islands + South Africa
  • Year 6: South Atlantic + Brazil + Azores + back to Europe
  • Year 7: Mediterranean + North Atlantic summer (optional) + completion

Timeline

Year 1 Mediterranean (test season) + winter refit

May–Oct (Med)

Test runs, systems shake‑down, crew procedures, and coastal/offshore legs.

  • Suggested grounds: Balearics → Corsica/Sardinia → Tyrrhenian → Greece
  • Deliverables: spares strategy, maintenance playbook, safety drills

Nov–Mar (Europe)

Yard time & refit window in Europe (weather + schedule friendly).

  • Hull/rig inspections, service cycles, electronics updates
  • Winter living or lay‑up depending on programme
Year 2 Med + Atlantic crossing + Caribbean

Apr–Oct (Med)

First “proper run” — longer legs, bigger weather windows, offshore confidence.

  • Training: night sailing, heavy‑weather protocols, watch systems
  • Prep: inventory, storm shutters, ditch bag, comms tests

Nov–Apr (Atlantic → Caribbean)

Atlantic crossing in the traditional late‑autumn window, then winter Caribbean.

  • Canaries / Cape Verde → St Lucia / Grenada
  • Leewards & Windwards during peak trade‑wind season
Year 3 Panama + Galápagos + South Pacific (dry season) + NZ

Jan–Oct

  • Jan–Mar: Caribbean → Panama staging
  • Mar–Apr: Panama Canal transit, provisioning
  • Apr–May: Galápagos (optional) → Marquesas
  • May–Oct: French Polynesia, Cooks, Tonga (best weather)

Nov–Mar (Cyclone avoidance)

Move outside the tropical cyclone zone for the core season: New Zealand (or southern Australia) is a common strategy.

  • Maintenance + upgrades while still cruising
  • Great sailing in NZ summer (Dec–Feb)
Year 4 Australia + Indonesia + SE Asia

Apr–Oct (Coral Sea / GBR)

Queensland / Great Barrier Reef during the calmer window.

  • Whitsundays, Lizard Island, Torres Strait planning
  • Systems: heat management, spares, remote logistics

Oct–Mar (Indonesia → Thailand/Malaysia)

Transition west through Indonesia, then base in SE Asia for the northern winter. (Monsoon patterns vary by sub‑region — refine per exact route.)

  • Komodo / Banda Sea / Raja Ampat (route-dependent)
  • Andaman Sea, Phuket/Langkawi base for maintenance
Year 5 Indian Ocean islands + South Africa

May–Sep (Indian Ocean)

  • Maldives / Sri Lanka → Chagos (permits) → Seychelles
  • Madagascar (route & timing dependent)
  • Mauritius / Réunion (weather window dependent)

Oct–Mar (South Africa)

Arrive for the southern summer: Cape Town and the coast.

  • Maintenance + spares + crew rotations
  • Prepare for South Atlantic crossing
Year 6 South Atlantic + Brazil + Azores + Europe

Nov–Feb (South Atlantic)

  • Cape Town → St Helena / Ascension → NE Brazil
  • Brazil coast cruising (seasonal planning required)

May–Aug (Azores → Europe)

Work north for the Azores, then Europe for a summer arrival — avoiding the core Atlantic hurricane season.

  • Azores staging
  • Portugal/Spain → Mediterranean
Year 7 Mediterranean + North Atlantic summer (optional) + completion

May–Oct

  • Mediterranean “grand tour” (Italy → Greece → Turkey)
  • Or: optional North Atlantic (UK/Ireland/Norway) in high summer

Completion

Close the loop, refresh the plan, and decide the next chapter: repeat, pause, or reposition.

  • Full maintenance cycle + 5–10 year survey planning
  • Upgrade roadmap for the next 7 years
