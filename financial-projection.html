7-year circumnavigation concept

Around the World

A season-aware route designed to reduce exposure to hurricane and cyclone seasons while still spending the best months in the best cruising grounds.

Mediterranean: May–Oct Atlantic hurricanes: Jun–Nov South Pacific cyclones: Nov–Apr Australia cyclones: Nov–Apr SW Indian Ocean cyclones: mid‑Nov–Apr

Route overview

This is a “good‑weather‑first” concept. The idea: cross oceans during established windows, and spend core cyclone season outside the tropics (e.g., New Zealand / southern Australia).

Year 1
Mediterranean test summer
  • May–Oct: Balearics → Corsica/Sardinia → Italy → Greece
  • Nov–Mar: winter refit in Europe
Year 2
Med + Atlantic + Caribbean
  • Apr–Oct: Mediterranean (longer legs, training)
  • Nov–Apr: Atlantic crossing → Caribbean season
Year 3
Panama + Galápagos + South Pacific
  • Mar–May: Panama Canal + Galápagos (optional) → Marquesas
  • May–Oct: French Polynesia / Cooks / Tonga
  • Nov–Mar: cyclone avoidance in NZ
Year 4
Australia + Indonesia + SE Asia
  • Apr–Oct: Coral Sea / GBR
  • Oct–Mar: Indonesia → Thailand/Malaysia base
Year 5
Indian Ocean + South Africa
  • May–Sep: Maldives / Seychelles / Mauritius (route-dependent)
  • Oct–Mar: South Africa summer + maintenance
Year 6
South Atlantic + Europe
  • Nov–Feb: Cape Town → Brazil (via islands)
  • May–Aug: Azores → Europe summer arrival
Year 7
Mediterranean completion
  • May–Oct: Med grand tour (Italy → Greece → Turkey)
  • Optional: North Atlantic summer (UK/Ireland/Norway)
